Why the Amazon is Vital

The Amazon Rainforest is home to over 10% of the known species on Earth. Its trees produce about 20% of the world's oxygen, making it crucial for global climate regulation.

Conservation efforts are critical as deforestation continues to threaten this vital ecosystem. Protecting the Amazon helps preserve biodiversity, local communities, and climate stability.
